Biography

Shaohua Wang, Ph.D., is an Innovative Research Fellow at the Aerospace Information Research Institute, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and a master's advisor. He serves as a part-time researcher at the China Geographic Information Industry Association and also a part-time graduate supervisor at Lanzhou Jiaotong University.

His research focuses on spatiotemporal big data analysis, spatial analysis, and geographic spatial intelligence. His primary academic contributions lie in the field of CyberGIS, where his papers are ranked fourth internationally. He has developed a spatiotemporal big data analysis engine and technical architecture, along with a geographical information system built on autonomously controllable software and hardware. This system has facilitated a series of methods aimed at accelerating solutions to spatial optimization problems by reducing search space.

Shaohua Wang has taken the lead in 6 projects at the provincial and ministerial levels and has actively participated in over 10 national and provincial-level scientific research projects. He boasts a portfolio of over 100 academic papers, both domestic and international, with more than 50 of them being authored as the first or corresponding author. Additionally, he holds 8 authorized and pending invention patents.

His achievements include receiving the Beijing Science and Technology First Prize in 2015, the Special Prize of China Geographic Information Technology Progress Award in 2014, and the 7th Outstanding Paper Award from the Beijing Standardization Association in 2013.He is a council member of the International Chinese Association of Geographic Information Science (CPGIS) and a committee member of the Resource Mapping Professional Committee of the China Society of Natural Resources.
